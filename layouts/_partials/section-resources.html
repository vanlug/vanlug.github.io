Logo

From 1999 to 2026, VanLUG used an unmodified Tux{{ partial "icon" "external" }} by Larry Ewing, Simon Budig and Garrett LeSage as its icon, paired with a stylized mountain logo created by Ben Holt.

The current icon, adopted in 2026, is a modified Tux Flat SVG{{ partial "icon" "external" }} by gg3po and Iwan Gabovitch (based on NewTux.svg{{ partial "icon" "external" }} by gg3po, itself based on the original Tux.png{{ partial "icon" "external" }} by Larry Ewing) with the wavy stripes of the Vancouver flag added to the belly. It is licensed under the GNU GPL v2{{ partial "icon" "external" }} or later. The full logo pairs the icon with the “VanLUG / Vancouver Linux User Group” wordmark.
